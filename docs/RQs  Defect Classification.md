# **Proposed Research Questions**

| Dimension & Focus                                          | Proposed Research Question                                                                                                                                                                   | Academic Rationale & JSS Alignment                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| :--------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Dimension 1: Taxonomy Robustness & ODC Limitations**     | **RQ1.1:** What are the most prevalent semantic defect types within the Defects4J dataset, and how do these distributions correlate with the intrinsic nature of the source code?            | Aligns with JSS's preference for empirical characterization (similar to Catolino et al. 36). Identifying dominant types provides actionable insights for software testing prioritization.                                                                                   |
| _(Focus: Applicability of ODC to modern datasets)_         | **RQ1.2:** How does the explicit separation of defect "Impact" (e.g., performance degradation) from defect "Type" (e.g., algorithmic logic) influence the accuracy of automated triage?      | Directly answers the user's query regarding "performance bugs." Demonstrates deep theoretical knowledge of the IBM v5.2 standard and its superiority over symptom-only labeling.                                                                                            |
| **Dimension 2: Efficacy of the LLM Scientific Protocol**   | **RQ2.1:** How effective is an LLM-driven classification pipeline, guided by a scientific debugging protocol, in autonomously categorizing software defects into ODC types?                  | The core quantitative validation of the methodology. Tests overall precision, recall, and the self-consistency of the tool against human-expert benchmarks.                                                                                                                 |
| _(Focus: The technological contribution of AutoSD/Zeller)_ | **RQ2.2:** Does the integration of Zeller’s scientific debugging protocol (Hypothesize-Observe-Predict) significantly reduce LLM hallucination compared to unstructured zero-shot prompting? | Justifies the specific prompt engineering choices. It empirically proves that the AutoSD-inspired pipeline is a necessary architectural component, not a superficial addition.                                                                                              |
| _(Focus: Taxonomy grounding effect)_                       | **RQ2.3:** Does explicit ODC taxonomy grounding significantly improve classification consistency and actionability compared to unconstrained LLM defect classification?                      | Isolates the contribution of the taxonomy itself, independent of prompt engineering. Demonstrates that structured frameworks (ODC) reduce label drift and improve inter-analyst agreement — a key claim for JSS reviewers evaluating the practical utility of the approach. |
| **Dimension 3: Bridging the Semantic Gap**                 | **RQ3.1:** How does the semantic classification of a defect diverge when an LLM relies exclusively on pre-fix evidence (symptoms) versus post-fix evidence (repair diffs)?                   | The centerpiece of the evaluation defense. Directly investigates the "Evidence Asymmetry" pillar and explores the fundamental differences between diagnosis and autopsy.                                                                                                    |
| **Dimension 4: Scalability & Multi-Fault Interference**    | **RQ4.1:** What is the statistical inter-rater reliability (Cohen’s Kappa) between an effect-oriented diagnostic model (pre-fix) and a cause-oriented diagnostic model (post-fix)?           | Provides the rigorous statistical validation expected by Q1 journals. Moves the paper away from simple percentage-based accuracy into robust statistical methodology.                                                                                                       |
